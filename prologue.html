    Quote

    “You’re only given a little spark of madness, you mustn’t lose it.”

    Robin Williams in his Emmy Award Acceptance Speech

    Just moments before, the crowds and the pandemonium seemed a world away, even though all that separated him from them was the grand old curtain. Straightening his coat for the umpteenth time, a part of him wanted to shrivel up and enjoy that temporary peace for a little longer. But just then the curtains parted, and fear made way for wonderment, thrill and even a little hope. He could feel his unsure-self melting under the blinding lights, and he left it behind to step on stage. The noise was ecstatic, and the crowd loved him as much as he loved hearing them. With an ecstatic greeting he began, and their loud cheering just showed him that he was not going anywhere soon.

    When the clock struck 10, people across an entire country would tune themselves in every day to laugh, remember and enjoy the ‘mind’ candy that he had perfected. In this world where we take our comedians seriously and the politicians as a joke, his fame was almost undemocratic.

    The pen is mightier than the sword so it is said, but he had made his voice mightier than the pen, this was his power. To think that a comedian can lash at the politicians, to think that the tremors of his opinion could shake the very government, that was the extent of his success.

    This was his stage, no one dared to challenge a joker in his own joke. Not until the sadists had gathered and an ultimatum was passed, the mischievous act of the comedian was silenced forever. The unchallenged king of the laughter lost his life to envy and greed.

    Little did he know that night he would laugh his last laugh. Even the faint laughter of the people died down as they worded the only question on their mind. What had taken the laughter away?
